## Virtual Pet

{% include gallery id="virtualpet_gallery" caption="Pictures of the game Virtual Pet" %}

During my internship, I worked with a team of four to develop Virtual Pet, an Android game focused on taking care of a digital mascot. Inspired by games like My Talking Tom, Pou and Tamagotchi, this project ended up being my first real step into game development.

In the game, players can take care of their pet by feeding it, giving it water, bathing it, and even giving it medicine. You can also pet it as much as you want and play a couple of mini-games to earn coins and keep things fun and interactive.

We put a lot of emphasis on making the mascots feel as cute and expressive as possible using Blender. I was responsible for creating one of the pets, a lynx. I started by modeling the body with a soft, appealing look, then worked on adding the characteristic features that make a lynx recognizable while still keeping that stylized, friendly vibe.

After modeling, I moved on to rigging so the character could be animated properly. Using Non-Linear Animation, I created different animations to represent the pet’s moods and reactions, which helped make it feel more alive and responsive.

Moving into Unity, I worked on designing the UI and implementing the logic behind it. I also developed most of the pet’s core functionalities, such as eating, drinking, and taking medicine. It was a really fun experience overall, even if it came with its challenges, especially being my first game. That said, it’s exactly that challenge that made the project so rewarding and confirmed my interest in game development.

## Grouplanner

{% include gallery id="grouplanner_gallery" caption="Pictures of the Grouplanner app. All pictures displayed here are used with permission." %}

From November 2024 to November 2025, I worked as a full-stack developer on Grouplanner, a group recommendation mobile application for tourism. The app is designed to generate personalized travel suggestions based on users’ personality, which is determined through a quiz.

Grouplanner allows users to plan trips either individually or in groups. When planning as a group, the system analyzes each member’s personality and generates recommendations that either suit the group as a whole or intelligently divides users into subgroups with similar preferences. In addition, the app integrates contextual notifications, providing users with real-time information about nearby points of interest, as well as environmental factors like the weather, air quality, and noise levels.

Gamification plays a central role in the app's experience. Users earn XP, coins, and trophies by interacting with the app, which contribute to their progression and engagement. Coins are also connected to the integrated Virtual Pet system, where in-game currency can be used to care for the user’s chosen companion.

My main responsibility within the project was the implementation of contextual notifications, ensuring that relevant information was delivered to users based on their preferences and surroundings. Alongside this, I worked on embedding the Unity-based Virtual Pet game into the Android application, connecting the gap between the standalone game and the main platform.

I also contributed to both frontend and backend development. On the frontend side, I helped rebuild the application structure. On the backend, I was involved in designing API endpoints, implementing business logic, and creating and maintaining databases to support the system’s functionality.

The project was developed by a team of five, working under an agile methodology with weekly meetings, task distribution, and continuous iteration. This environment allowed me to gain hands-on experience in collaborative software development, while also strengthening my ability to manage responsibilities within a structured workflow.

As my first full-scale experience as a software engineer, this project was both challenging and extremely rewarding after the results.
